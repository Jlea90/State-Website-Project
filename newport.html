Historic
Charm

Nestled along the shores of Rhode Island, Newport exudes an air of historic charm, with its grand mansions standing as timeless sentinels to an opulent past. The Gilded Age lives on in these stately homes, where stories of bygone eras whisper through the halls. Strolling down Bellevue Avenue, you can feel the legacy of Astors and Vanderbilts, and the architectural splendor that has been meticulously preserved. Quaint shops and boutiques are peppered throughout the city, offering a delightful mix of modern luxury and vintage finds, inviting visitors to experience a piece of history while indulging in some retail therapy.

Ocean
Drive

Newport's Ocean Drive is a breathtaking stretch of scenic coastline that captures the essence of Rhode Island's maritime beauty. As you cruise along the winding road, you'll be treated to stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean, rocky shorelines, and sprawling estates that echo the elegance of Newport's past. Beyond the picturesque views, Ocean Drive leads you to quaint neighborhoods and bustling areas filled with unique shops and boutiques. Here, visitors can find everything from nautical-themed decor to locally crafted jewelry, offering a perfect blend of coastal charm and sophisticated shopping. Whether you're browsing seaside trinkets or indulging in some retail therapy, Ocean Drive’s mix of natural beauty and delightful stores promises an unforgettable experience that captures the spirit of Newport.

Facts about Newport, RI

  • Population: Approximately 25,000 residents
  • Incorporated: 1784
  • Region: Located in the southeastern region of Rhode Island, on Aquidneck Island
  • Classification: Urban city with a vibrant seaside community
  • Average Income Level: Median household income around $81,330, slightly below the state average
