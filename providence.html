Renaissance
City

Providence, Rhode Island, is a city rich in history and vibrant urban life. Established in 1636 by Roger Williams and incorporated in 1832, Providence is one of the oldest cities in New England and the capital of Rhode Island. Known as the "Renaissance City," it lies in the northern region of the state and is celebrated for its artistic, cultural, and educational significance. With a population nearing 191,000, Providence is the largest city in the state and is classified as an urban center, reflecting its bustling, metropolitan character.

Thayer
Street

Thayer Street in Providence, RI, is a vibrant and eclectic hub that perfectly captures the city’s youthful energy and cultural diversity. Nestled alongside prestigious institutions like Brown University and the Rhode Island School of Design, Thayer Street is brimming with lively cafes, unique boutiques, and trendy restaurants that cater to all tastes and styles. Visitors can wander down this bustling street and find everything from vintage bookstores to quirky gift shops, all offering one-of-a-kind treasures. Food lovers are in for a treat, too – from artisanal ice cream to globally-inspired street food, Thayer Street’s culinary scene is a feast for the senses. Whether you’re in the mood for a cozy coffee shop to relax in, a cutting-edge art gallery to explore, or a new favorite restaurant to discover, Thayer Street’s charm and creativity promise something unforgettable for every visitor. Come soak up the artsy, welcoming vibe and see for yourself why Thayer Street is a beloved local gem and a must-visit for anyone in Providence!

Facts about Providence, RI

  • Population: Approximately 191,000 residents
  • Incorporated: 1832
  • Region: Located in the northern part of Rhode Island
  • Classification: Urban city and the capital of Rhode Island
  • Average Income Level: Slightly below the state average, with median household income around $64,000
